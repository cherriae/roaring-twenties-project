The Harlem Renaissance
Art as Resistance:
How the Harlem Renaissance Redefined Black Identity in the 1920s

Why it Matters

The Harlem Renaissance (1920s–30s) was a cultural explosion in which Black Americans redefined their identity through art, literature, music, and activism — creating safe spaces for marginalized communities, including LGBTQ+ artists who shaped the entire movement.

Supporting Details

  • Jazz & Blues transformed American music at the Cotton Club & Apollo Theater
  • Literary salons gave voice to Black writers and poets
  • A'Lelia Walker's Dark Tower was an openly inclusive safe space
  • Figures like Bessie Smith & Gladys Bentley lived openly queer lives
