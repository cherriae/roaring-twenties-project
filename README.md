# The Harlem Renaissance: Reclaiming Identity Through Art & Expression

An interactive 2D pixel-art experience exploring the cultural explosion of the Harlem Renaissance in 1920s Harlem, New York.

## Theme

The Harlem Renaissance was a transformative cultural movement where Black Americans — including LGBTQ+ artists, musicians, and writers — reclaimed their identity through an unprecedented flowering of art, literature, and music. This project highlights how spaces like A'Lelia Walker's Dark Tower and the Cotton Club became havens for creative expression and self-determination.

## Key Locations (9 Areas)

- **The Cotton Club** — Jazz club featuring Louis Armstrong and Duke Ellington
- **Apollo Theater** — Bessie Smith and Ma Rainey perform the blues
- **Literary Salon** — Langston Hughes, Zora Neale Hurston, and bookshelves
- **A'Lelia Walker's Dark Tower** — A safe space for all artists, including LGBTQ+ figures like Richard Bruce Nugent, Countee Cullen
- **The Clam House (Speakeasy)** — Gladys Bentley performs; Alain Locke discusses philosophy
- **Rent Party House** — Claude McKay and communal gatherings
- **Abyssinian Baptist Church** — Rev. Powell discusses the Great Migration
- **Marcus Garvey Park** — Marcus Garvey and W.E.B. Du Bois debate Black liberation
- **Outdoor Art Gallery** — Aaron Douglas and Augusta Savage showcase visual art

## Historical Figures (16 Interactive NPCs)

Louis Armstrong, Duke Ellington, Langston Hughes, Zora Neale Hurston, A'Lelia Walker, Richard Bruce Nugent, Countee Cullen, Gladys Bentley, Alain Locke, Bessie Smith, Ma Rainey, Aaron Douglas, Augusta Savage, Marcus Garvey, W.E.B. Du Bois, Rev. Adam Clayton Powell Sr.

## Features

- **3600×2800 pixel world** with scrolling camera
- **Pixel-art characters** with proper proportions (head, body, limbs, hats)
- **Multi-page dialogue** system — each NPC has 3-4 pages of historically accurate info
- **120+ ambient NPCs** — dancers, writers, strollers, churchgoers
- **Mini-map** with all locations marked
- **Neon signs**, flickering marquee lights, parked cars, trees, street lamps
- **Historical info plaques** along the streets

## How to Run

1. Open this folder in VS Code.
2. Run `python -m http.server 8000` in the terminal (or use the Run Server task).
3. Open `http://localhost:8000` in your browser.

## Controls

- **WASD / Arrow Keys**: Move
- **SPACE**: Talk to NPCs (multi-page — keep pressing SPACE to read more)
